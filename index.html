Introduction

Web browsers have been the gateway to the internet for decades. They interpret HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to present web pages to users. Over the years, browsers have evolved rapidly in terms of performance, compatibility, and features.

Learn more about how web browsers work.

A brief history of IE

Internet Explorer was once the dominant web browser, especially in the early 2000s. Developed by Microsoft, it came preinstalled with Windows and achieved widespread adoption.

Visit Microsoft Edge to see its successor.

A brief history of Firefox

Firefox was developed by the Mozilla Foundation and became popular as an open-source alternative to Internet Explorer. It focused on user privacy and customization through extensions.

Explore more at the official Firefox website.

A brief history of Chrome

Google Chrome launched in 2008 and quickly gained popularity for its speed and clean interface. It's now the most widely used browser in the world and serves as the foundation for many others via the Chromium project.
